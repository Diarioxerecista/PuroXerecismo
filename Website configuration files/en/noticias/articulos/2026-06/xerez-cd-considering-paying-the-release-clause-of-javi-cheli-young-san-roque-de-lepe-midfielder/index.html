Xerez CD considering paying the release clause of Javi Cheli, young San Roque de Lepe midfielder

According to sources close to the club, the blue-and-white sporting management are exploring a move for the sub-23 midfielder, one of last season's breakout performers in Tercera RFEF.

Xerez Club Deportivo are reportedly considering paying the release clause of Javi Cheli, a young midfielder at CD San Roque de Lepe, according to sources close to the club. If confirmed, the move would not yet have official backing from either party.

Cheli, a sub-23 player and son of the former Recreativo de Huelva player of the same nickname, had a notable season in Tercera RFEF with the Lepe-based club, where he remains under contract until 2027. His strong form, marked by his ability to arrive from midfield and his composure on the ball, has attracted interest from several higher-division clubs.

San Roque de Lepe have already publicly acknowledged that the midfielder's future is not settled, and that he could leave if a club is willing to pay the clause set out in his contract. Among the clubs reportedly interested in the player, according to reports in Huelva-based media, are Recreativo de Huelva, Cádiz CF and even RCD Mallorca.

Should the move go through, Cheli's arrival would represent a bet on young local talent, in a season in which Xerez CD continue working to strengthen their midfield ahead of the start of the competition.
